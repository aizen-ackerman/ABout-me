About Me

The Science of Systems →
Working with Linux systems and low-level programs to grep the hidden spaces looks fun — but for me, it’s about understanding why those spaces exist in the first place.

I spend time exploring how systems behave beyond their documented design. By breaking assumptions, tracing execution paths, and observing unintended behavior, I learn how security weaknesses surface in real environments.

I am a Computer Science student with a focused interest in cybersecurity, system internals, and low-level analysis. I regularly work on CTF challenges involving reverse engineering, binary exploitation, and web security to understand real-world attack surfaces.

My technical background includes Java problem solving, Python scripting, Linux environments, and hands-on debugging and analysis. I enjoy building small tools, experimenting with ideas, and documenting what I learn through notes and writeups.

This space serves as a living record of my learning process, experiments, and practical exploration in security and systems.
